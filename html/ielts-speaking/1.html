[001] HOMETOWN

🎧 Where are you from?

I'm from a small village on the outskirts of Hanoi. It's a really peaceful and quiet place. Even though I've been living in the city for the past four years for my studies, I still go back to my hometown on weekends whenever I want a break from the busy city life.

🎧 What do you like about your hometown?

What I really like about my hometown is the hospitality of the people. In my village, everyone lives close together and is always willing to lend a hand whenever someone needs help. That's something I rarely find in the area where I live now, where neighbours often treat each other like strangers.

🎧 What is it like where you live?

I live in central Hanoi, where there are plenty of townhouse complexes and high-rise buildings. These housing developments are designed to accommodate the huge number of people moving to the city in search of job opportunities.

🎧 Do you like living in the city?

To be honest, I don't really enjoy living in the city, even though it offers a lot of modern facilities and conveniences. I prefer being surrounded by nature, trees, and spacious yards. What I dislike the most is the traffic. Sometimes it's a complete nightmare, especially when I get stuck in traffic jams for hours.

🎧 Would you prefer to live somewhere else? (Why)?

Absolutely. If I had the chance, I'd prefer to live in my hometown village. However, in the long run, I'll probably continue living in the city because there are more job opportunities here. Maybe after I retire, I'll move back to my hometown and settle down there for good.

🎧 Does your hometown have a lot of tourists?

No, it doesn't. It's just a typical countryside village in Vietnam, with small houses, winding alleys, rice fields, and rivers. It's quite an ordinary place, so there aren't really any tourist attractions there

🎧 Do you think your hometown is a good place for young people? (Why)?

Actually, I don't think my hometown is an ideal place for young people. Public facilities there are still underdeveloped. For example, there aren't many universities, hospitals, shopping centres, or large companies like there are in big cities. On top of that, public transportation is quite limited. I think young people are better off moving to the city, where they have more opportunities to find a good job and enjoy a more modern lifestyle

🎧 In what ways can you improve your hometown?

Well, although living conditions in my hometown have improved significantly over time, there still aren't many entertainment facilities. For example, we have to travel about 30 kilometres to the city just to watch a movie at the cinema or go shopping at a mall. Sometimes it can be really frustrating because heavy traffic can make the bus journey take hours. That's why I hope more public facilities such as cinemas, shopping malls, gyms, and museums will be built in my hometown, so people can enjoy their free time more conveniently.

🎧 What is the main reason for liking a hometown other than the fact you were born there?

Well, being born and raised in a hometown is probably the main reason people feel attached to it. However, that connection also comes from many other factors. Over the years, we become familiar with the lifestyle there and create both happy and sad memories that stay with us forever. Our loved ones are also there, such as our family, friends, first love, and teachers. In other words, most of our childhood memories are deeply connected to our hometown, which is why we often feel nostalgic when we've been away from it for a long time.

🎧 Most people in this world do not live in their hometowns. Why?

A hometown is usually a comfortable place where people are surrounded by family and friends. It can be seen as a comfort zone where we feel safe and secure. However, for many people, staying in that environment may make it harder for them to pursue bigger career goals. There's a saying that “life begins at the end of your comfort zone,” so many people choose to leave their hometown to explore new places and build their careers. It's also a great opportunity to experience different cultures and ways of life around the world.

🎧 Some people want to live in their hometowns for the rest of their lives. Why?

I think some people want to live in their hometowns for the rest of their lives because they don't want to leave their comfort zone. They've become very familiar with the environment, including their family, neighbours, close friends, and daily routines. Moving to a new place means having to start everything from scratch, which can feel challenging and risky. That's why many people prefer to settle down in their hometown permanently.

🎧 Which affects a person's personality more, the hometown or the current city? Why?

Personally, I don't think personality is fixed. It changes and develops over time depending on our experiences and surroundings. That's why I believe the place where we currently live can have a stronger influence on our personality. For example, when I was a child, I was quite shy and reserved. However, after moving to the city for my studies a few years ago, I gradually became more outgoing in order to adapt to the fast-paced lifestyle there.

🎧 Do people with the same hometown have a similar thinking? Why?

Yes and no. I believe that everyone has their own way of thinking. Even within the same community, some people may share similar opinions while others may have completely different viewpoints. For example, in my family, my father and I are usually on the same wavelength, whereas my mother tends to agree more with my brother. So, I don't think it's possible for everyone from the same hometown to think alike.

[002] WORK / JOB

🎧 Do you work or study?

I'm currently working as a content writer at a small private English center. My job mainly involves creating and compiling learning materials for English students. It's a role I enjoy because it allows me to contribute to the learning process and help students improve their language skills

🎧 Why did you choose to do that type of work?

I chose this job because, even though it doesn't pay very well, I find it quite rewarding. I've always had a strong interest in education, so being involved in helping students learn is something I genuinely enjoy. What motivates me the most is seeing students improve over time. Knowing that I've played a small part in their progress gives me a real sense of satisfaction

🎧 What do you like about your job?

What I like most about my job is that it allows me to support myself and live independently in the city. I also enjoy the working environment. The facilities at the center are modern and well-equipped, and my manager and colleagues are all friendly and supportive. Overall, it's a pleasant place to work, and I feel comfortable being part of the team

🎧 What do you dislike about your job?

For the most part, I'd say the workload is what I dislike the most. There are always multiple projects going on at the same time, so we're often under a lot of pressure to meet deadlines. That said, the work itself is fairly straightforward, so we can usually handle it and get everything done on time

🎧 Is that a popular job in your country?

Yes, I'd say being a content creator is quite a popular career choice in my country, especially among young people. With the growth of social media platforms, many people are attracted to this job because it offers creativity and flexibility. However, not everyone finds it appealing. Some people think it's boring because creating content regularly for months or even years can become repetitive and monotonous

🎧 Is there anything special about your job?

Yes, I think one of the most special things about my job is the freedom it gives me to be creative. I'm encouraged to work in different environments, such as a book café or other quiet places, to find inspiration and come up with fresh ideas. Another thing I really appreciate is the flexibility. Sometimes, I can work from home, which makes my work-life balance much more comfortable. On top of that, my manager is very easy-going and supportive, so the working environment is quite relaxed and enjoyable

🎧 Would you recommend your current job to other people?

Yes, I definitely would. In fact, I've already recommended this job to several of my friends who studied in the same English department as I did at university. I think it's a rewarding career because it allows people to be creative and constantly come up with new ideas. It also helps develop imagination and communication skills, which can be valuable in many different areas of life and work

🎧 What do you do after work?

After work, I usually head home, take a shower, and prepare myself a meal. Once I've had dinner, I like to relax on the sofa and watch some TV to unwind after a long day. Sometimes, I read a book or listen to music, but most of the time I end up watching TV. Later in the evening, before going to bed, I check my emails and social media. However, I prefer to stay away from my computer for a few hours right after work so I can properly relax and recharge

🎧 Do you miss being a student?

Yes, absolutely. I often miss being a student because life felt much simpler back then. My main responsibility was studying, and everything was well organized by teachers who were experienced and supportive. I also had plenty of opportunities to learn new things and try different activities without having to worry too much about work or financial responsibilities. Looking back, student life felt quite carefree, which is something I really appreciate now that I'm working full-time

🎧 Which jobs would you say are most respected in your country?

That's an interesting question. I've never really thought about it before, but I'd say that careers in business, education, and healthcare are among the most respected in my country. People in the business sector are often admired because they can achieve financial success and enjoy a high social status. On the other hand, teachers, doctors, and nurses earn respect for a different reason. They dedicate their time and effort to educating people and taking care of patients, which has a positive impact on society. In Vietnam, these professions are generally held in very high regard because of the valuable contributions they make to the community

🎧 Some people say it's better to work for yourself than be employed by a company. What's your opinion?

I think it really depends on the individual. Some people are naturally entrepreneurial and successfully start their own businesses soon after graduating from university. However, for me personally, I believe working for a company is an important first step before becoming self-employed. By working as an employee, I can gain practical experience, learn how a business operates, and better understand the challenges employees face in the workplace. This experience can be extremely valuable if I decide to run my own business in the future. It would help me become a more understanding and effective manager because I'd know how to treat my staff fairly and build positive working relationships with them. In my opinion, when employees feel respected and supported, a business has a much better chance of growing and succeeding

🎧 What changes in employment have there been in recent years in your country?

There have been quite a few changes in employment in my country over the past few years. In the past, many people from rural areas moved to big cities to look for better job opportunities. While this still happens today, the trend has changed to some extent. Nowadays, more companies and industrial parks are being developed in suburban and rural areas, which allows people to find jobs closer to where they live instead of relocating to major cities. Another noticeable change is the growing interest in entrepreneurship among young people. More and more young adults are starting their own businesses or working on innovative projects. As a result, the start-up scene has become much more active and dynamic than it was in the past

🎧 What are some of the important things a candidate should find out before accepting a job?

I think everyone has their own priorities when choosing a job. Personally, one of the first things I would look at is the compensation package, including the salary, bonuses, insurance, annual leave, and any other benefits the company offers. Besides that, I would want to have a clear understanding of my responsibilities and expectations before accepting the position. This helps ensure that the workload is reasonable and prevents misunderstandings later on. I'd also pay attention to the working environment and opportunities for career growth, as these factors can have a big impact on long-term job satisfaction

🎧 Why do you think some people choose to do difficult jobs?

I can think of several reasons why some people choose to do difficult jobs. First of all, some people are motivated by a sense of responsibility and want to contribute to society. For example, firefighters, police officers, and emergency workers often choose these careers because they want to help others and make a positive difference in their communities. Another reason is that some people simply enjoy challenges and exciting experiences. They tend to perform well under pressure and are comfortable taking risks, so demanding jobs can be more appealing to them than routine office work. Finally, financial rewards can also be a major factor. In many cases, the more difficult, stressful, or dangerous a job is, the higher the salary tends to be. As a result, some people are willing to take on these challenges in exchange for better pay and career opportunities

🎧 Is it always necessary to work hard in order to achieve career success?

I don't think working hard alone is enough to guarantee career success. Of course, hard work is important because it helps people develop skills and achieve their goals. However, I believe working smart is just as important, if not more so. People who are successful often know how to prioritize tasks, manage their time effectively, and focus on activities that create the greatest value. Instead of spending long hours on unnecessary work, they look for efficient ways to achieve better results. For that reason, I think the ideal approach is to combine hard work with smart decision-making. When people work both hard and smart, they are much more likely to succeed in their careers

🎧 Do you think that successful people are always happy people?

That's an interesting question. I think it really depends on how we define success. If success is measured mainly by wealth, status, or professional achievements, then I would say the answer is no. Having a lot of money or a successful career doesn't automatically make someone happy. In fact, some highly successful people struggle with stress, loneliness, or maintaining a healthy work-life balance. On the other hand, if we take a broader view of success, the answer could be yes. Success can also mean achieving personal goals, building meaningful relationships, maintaining good health, or spending quality time with family. When people succeed in these areas of life, they are often happier and more satisfied. So, in my opinion, success can contribute to happiness, but it depends on what kind of success we're talking about

Extended families still exist, but it's less common for all family members to live under the same roof or even in the same neighborhood. I think it's much more flexible nowadays than it used to be. However, things have changed significantly over the years. More and more women are pursuing careers and contributing equally to the family's income. As a result, important family decisions are often made jointly by both husband and wife. In my opinion, there isn't always a single family leader anymore. Instead, many couples share responsibilities and discuss major issues together before making decisions, which creates a more balanced and equal family relationship

🎧 In what ways is family life different now to the past? / In what ways have families in your country changed in recent years?

Families in my country have changed quite a lot over the past few decades, especially in terms of family size and family roles. In the past, it was common for several generations to live together under one roof. However, nowadays nuclear families, which consist only of parents and their children, have become much more common in both urban and rural areas. One reason for this change is that younger people often prefer to live independently, and separate households can help reduce conflicts caused by differences in lifestyle and opinions between generations. Another major change is the role of family members. In the past, fathers were usually the main breadwinners, while mothers were mainly responsible for raising children and doing housework. Today, both parents often work full-time, so responsibilities such as earning money, taking care of children, and managing household chores are shared more equally between them
