[001] HOMETOWN

🎧 Where are you from?

I'm from a small village on the outskirts of Hanoi. It's a really peaceful and quiet place. Even though I've been living in the city for the past four years for my studies, I still go back to my hometown on weekends whenever I want a break from the busy city life.

🎧 What do you like about your hometown?

What I really like about my hometown is the hospitality of the people. In my village, everyone lives close together and is always willing to lend a hand whenever someone needs help. That's something I rarely find in the area where I live now, where neighbours often treat each other like strangers.

🎧 What is it like where you live?

I live in central Hanoi, where there are plenty of townhouse complexes and high-rise buildings. These housing developments are designed to accommodate the huge number of people moving to the city in search of job opportunities.

🎧 Do you like living in the city?

To be honest, I don't really enjoy living in the city, even though it offers a lot of modern facilities and conveniences. I prefer being surrounded by nature, trees, and spacious yards. What I dislike the most is the traffic. Sometimes it's a complete nightmare, especially when I get stuck in traffic jams for hours.

🎧 Would you prefer to live somewhere else? (Why)?

Absolutely. If I had the chance, I'd prefer to live in my hometown village. However, in the long run, I'll probably continue living in the city because there are more job opportunities here. Maybe after I retire, I'll move back to my hometown and settle down there for good.

🎧 Does your hometown have a lot of tourists?

No, it doesn't. It's just a typical countryside village in Vietnam, with small houses, winding alleys, rice fields, and rivers. It's quite an ordinary place, so there aren't really any tourist attractions there

🎧 Do you think your hometown is a good place for young people? (Why)?

Actually, I don't think my hometown is an ideal place for young people. Public facilities there are still underdeveloped. For example, there aren't many universities, hospitals, shopping centres, or large companies like there are in big cities. On top of that, public transportation is quite limited. I think young people are better off moving to the city, where they have more opportunities to find a good job and enjoy a more modern lifestyle

🎧 In what ways can you improve your hometown?

Well, although living conditions in my hometown have improved significantly over time, there still aren't many entertainment facilities. For example, we have to travel about 30 kilometres to the city just to watch a movie at the cinema or go shopping at a mall. Sometimes it can be really frustrating because heavy traffic can make the bus journey take hours. That's why I hope more public facilities such as cinemas, shopping malls, gyms, and museums will be built in my hometown, so people can enjoy their free time more conveniently.

🎧 What is the main reason for liking a hometown other than the fact you were born there?

Well, being born and raised in a hometown is probably the main reason people feel attached to it. However, that connection also comes from many other factors. Over the years, we become familiar with the lifestyle there and create both happy and sad memories that stay with us forever. Our loved ones are also there, such as our family, friends, first love, and teachers. In other words, most of our childhood memories are deeply connected to our hometown, which is why we often feel nostalgic when we've been away from it for a long time.

🎧 Most people in this world do not live in their hometowns. Why?

A hometown is usually a comfortable place where people are surrounded by family and friends. It can be seen as a comfort zone where we feel safe and secure. However, for many people, staying in that environment may make it harder for them to pursue bigger career goals. There's a saying that “life begins at the end of your comfort zone,” so many people choose to leave their hometown to explore new places and build their careers. It's also a great opportunity to experience different cultures and ways of life around the world.

🎧 Some people want to live in their hometowns for the rest of their lives. Why?

I think some people want to live in their hometowns for the rest of their lives because they don't want to leave their comfort zone. They've become very familiar with the environment, including their family, neighbours, close friends, and daily routines. Moving to a new place means having to start everything from scratch, which can feel challenging and risky. That's why many people prefer to settle down in their hometown permanently.

🎧 Which affects a person's personality more, the hometown or the current city? Why?

Personally, I don't think personality is fixed. It changes and develops over time depending on our experiences and surroundings. That's why I believe the place where we currently live can have a stronger influence on our personality. For example, when I was a child, I was quite shy and reserved. However, after moving to the city for my studies a few years ago, I gradually became more outgoing in order to adapt to the fast-paced lifestyle there.

Do people with the same hometown have a similar thinking? Why?

Yes and no. I believe that everyone has their own way of thinking. Even within the same community, some people may share similar opinions while others may have completely different viewpoints. For example, in my family, my father and I are usually on the same wavelength, whereas my mother tends to agree more with my brother. So, I don't think it's possible for everyone from the same hometown to think alike.
