MY HOME

I live in an apartment complex called Vision in Tan Tao Ward, Binh Tan District. I bought my apartment about a year ago, so I've been living there ever since. Overall, I'm really happy with it.

The whole project is divided into three phases. So far, only the first phase has been completed, with four buildings named A, B, C, and D. Two of them are 25 stories tall, and the other two have 19 floors. The remaining six buildings are still only at the foundation stage, so I think it'll be quite a while before the entire project is finished. Once it's completed, there will be ten buildings altogether.

I live on the ninth floor of Building A, which is one of the 25-story buildings. Each building has two elevators, but to be honest, they've broken down quite a few times since I moved in. A lot of residents also complained that the elevators got really hot, especially during busy hours, so the management recently installed some extra fans inside. They're much more comfortable now.

One of my favorite things about living here is how spacious the apartment complex is. There are wide internal roads, a nice park with lots of trees and benches, and a large pond right in the middle. I often go for a walk there in the evening because it's quiet and a great way to relax after work.

One of the nicest parts of the apartment complex is the large open space in the middle of the four buildings. It's built slightly higher than the internal roads, and there are a few sets of stairs leading up to it. The area is filled with benches, so it's a popular place for residents to relax. In the evenings, it's usually full of people. Kids run around and play together, while adults sit on the benches, chat with their neighbors, or simply enjoy the fresh air. It creates a really friendly and lively atmosphere.

There are also two convenience stores on the ground floor, WinMart and Co.op Food, so buying groceries is usually very convenient. I only go to GO An Lac or AEON Mall when I need something that these stores don't have or when the prices there are noticeably cheaper.

The cafés downstairs are another nice feature. They're usually quite busy in the evenings, with people stopping by for coffee, chatting with friends, or even working on their laptops for a while

The apartment complex also has a large swimming pool, and the best part is that it's free for residents. It's a nice place to cool off on hot days or spend some time with family on the weekends.

Because the apartment complex has a lot of open space, there's even a basketball court. On normal days, people use it to play basketball, especially in the evenings. During special occasions, like International Children's Day on June 1st, the court is transformed into an event area where tents are set up and parties are held for children. It's always a fun and lively event, and a lot of families join in.

Another thing I like is the location. It's only about a seven-minute drive to GO An Lac Supermarket and around ten minutes to AEON Mall Binh Tan, so it's really convenient whenever I need to go shopping or grab something to eat.

The area around the apartment has improved a lot recently, too. In the past, the road leading to the complex was often flooded whenever there were high tides. But after both sides of the Tham Luong Canal were reinforced, flooding has become much less of an issue. The road used to be crowded with street vendors as well, which made driving in and out a bit frustrating. Thankfully, most of them have been cleared away, so getting around is much easier now.

One thing I appreciate is that there are quite a lot of security guards, and they seem to do their job very well. Because of that, I generally feel quite safe living here.

Besides those advantages, there are a couple of practical issues that residents often talk about.

The apartment complex only has one basement parking area. It's large enough for bicycles and motorbikes, but there's not enough space for cars. Even the parking spots along the internal roads are completely full, and the waiting lists for both the basement and the internal-road parking are very long. Last time I heard, each list had more than a hundred people waiting for a slot.

The downside is that there are quite a lot of mosquitoes around the park and the open areas in the late afternoon. On rainy days, rove beetles sometimes fly into the apartment through the balcony or windows, so I have to be careful and make sure they don't get inside.
